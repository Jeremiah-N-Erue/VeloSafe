# VeloSafe WhatsApp Community Playbook

How to run the founding riders and founding drivers WhatsApp groups without them becoming a liability.

---

## Group structure — set up before first invite goes out

### Two groups, not one

Create these separately:

1. **VeloSafe Founding Riders** — for waitlist riders
2. **VeloSafe Founding Drivers** — for driver applicants

Never mix them. Drivers in a rider group will pitch themselves; riders in a driver group will complain about hypotheticals. Separation is non-negotiable.

### Recommended settings (both groups)

- **Group type:** WhatsApp Community (not just a chat group) once you pass 100 members. Communities let you run multiple sub-groups later (announcements + discussion + regional).
- **Permissions:** Only admins can send messages (announcement-only mode). Open it up later once norms are established.
- **Group description:** Include a one-liner about what the group is, who's in it, what's allowed, and how to reach you outside the group.
- **Admins:** At least two admins. Never be a single point of failure. Start with you and a trusted community manager.
- **Group photo:** Use the VeloSafe shield logo on a white background. Keep it identical across both groups so members recognise your brand.

## Before launch — posting rhythm

### Weekly cadence

Aim for **one substantive post per week** in each group. Less and people forget you exist; more and it becomes noise. Good pre-launch posts:

- Behind-the-scenes updates (photos of your team, city visits, office)
- Milestone announcements (first driver verified, first safety partnership signed, first investor closed)
- Product screenshots or mockups asking for feedback
- Launch countdown starting at T-30 days
- Rider-specific: safety feature explainers, Lagos route stories
- Driver-specific: commission calculation examples, earnings projections, zone density maps

### What to never post

- Generic inspirational quotes
- News from other industries
- Reshared content from Bolt/Uber/etc. (even as critique — it signals obsession)
- Anything political or religious
- Anything that could be quoted out of context in a news article

### Tone

Conversational, specific, grounded. The voice should feel like a founder talking to early backers — not a brand pretending to be a friend. Use specific numbers ("we closed $80K this month"), specific names ("met with LASTMA this week"), and specific dates ("verification begins the second week of June").

## Moderation — what to remove and when

### Remove immediately, without apology

- Spam links
- Religious or political messages
- Unsolicited promotions from members
- Abusive or disrespectful messages
- Direct attacks on other members

### Handle privately first

- Disagreements with VeloSafe's direction (DM the member, address their concern)
- Complaints about application status (DM them with a real answer)
- Questions better answered 1-on-1

### Always respond in-group

- Product or feature questions
- Clarifications about commission, payouts, safety features
- General excitement or thanks

## Scaling — at what size things break

| Members | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| 0–50 | Run it yourself. Personal tone is an asset at this stage. |
| 50–250 | Add one community manager. Keep announcement-only mode. |
| 250–1,000 | Move to WhatsApp Communities. Create sub-groups by area (e.g. "Lekki Riders", "Ikeja Drivers"). |
| 1,000+ | You need a paid community manager. Consider Circle, Slack, or Discord for discussion; keep WhatsApp for announcements only. |

## Getting the invite link

Create each group first, then:

1. Open the group → Tap group name → **Invite via link**
2. Copy the link (starts with `https://chat.whatsapp.com/...`)
3. Paste it into `riders.html` and `drivers.html`, replacing `YOUR_RIDER_GROUP_INVITE` and `YOUR_DRIVER_GROUP_INVITE`
4. Paste the same links into your confirmation email templates

**Reset the invite link every 90 days** for security. If the link leaks publicly, you can reset it without losing existing members. Send a brief note when you reset.

## Data and privacy (NDPR compliance)

Members' phone numbers are visible to other members by default in WhatsApp groups. This is a legitimate privacy concern, especially for drivers whose vehicles can be identified.

**Options:**

1. **Acceptable risk** — disclose it in the group description, let members self-select. (Most common approach.)
2. **Hide numbers** — upgrade to WhatsApp Business and enable "Hide member phone numbers" in Community settings. This is the professional choice.
3. **WhatsApp Business API with a BSP** — overkill for pre-launch; consider for 1,000+ members.

Option 2 is the right answer for now. Costs nothing, takes 5 minutes, and is a real privacy upgrade over a standard group.

## What success looks like in the first 90 days

- 40%+ of email signups join the group (if lower, your confirmation email isn't prominent enough)
- Under 2% leave rate per month (if higher, your posting rhythm or tone is off)
- 5+ organic member-to-member conversations per week once discussion is opened
- Real DM feedback that ends up shaping product decisions
